Pollution

Data was obtained from the EPA which collects data about various pollutants through the use of air monitors placed in a variety of locations. Pollutant measures are expressed in terms of Air Quality Index (AQI).

Mortality

Data about mortality was obtained through the California Department of Public Health. Chronic lower respiratory disease, heart disease, stroke, and cancer were examined in this visualization to determine if there is a relation between air quality measurements and mortality per 100,000 people in various locations in California.

Pollution and Mortality

O3 is more prevalent when there is more sunlight so the summer months will have higher levels of O3. The other pollutants NO2, SO2, and CO more prevalent with more humidity so they are more prevalent in the winter months. Most of the pollutants affect respiratory diseases due to lung irritation. CO will affect the heart as well since it interferes with oxygen availability in the blood.
